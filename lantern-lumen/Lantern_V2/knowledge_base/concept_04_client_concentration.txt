METRIC: Client Concentration
CATEGORY: Risk

DEFINITION
Client concentration measures how dependent a company's revenue is on
a small number of clients. It answers the question: if our biggest
client left tomorrow, how badly would we be hurt? In professional
services, high client concentration is one of the most common and
underappreciated risks — a company can be profitable, growing, and
well-run, and still be one client departure away from a crisis.

HOW IT'S CALCULATED
Top Client Concentration = (Revenue from Top Client / Total Revenue) × 100
Top 3 Client Concentration = (Revenue from Top 3 Clients / Total Revenue) × 100

The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) can also be used for a more
complete picture, but top client percentage is the standard
advisory metric.

BENCHMARKS — PROFESSIONAL SERVICES INDUSTRY
Top Single Client Concentration:
  Safe:        Under 15%
  Acceptable:  15% to 25%
  Concerning:  25% to 40%
  Dangerous:   Above 40%

Top 3 Client Concentration:
  Safe:        Under 35%
  Acceptable:  35% to 50%
  Concerning:  50% to 65%
  Dangerous:   Above 65%

WHAT IT TELLS THE ADVISER
- How fragile the revenue base is
- Whether the company has genuine market traction or is dependent
  on a single relationship
- Whether the sales team is building a diversified pipeline or
  relying on one account
- How much negotiating leverage clients have (high concentration
  means clients know they have leverage)

WARNING SIGNS
- Single client above 30% of revenue — one decision by that client
  can destabilize the entire business
- Top client concentration increasing over time (the business is
  becoming more dependent, not less)
- A single client that is also the slowest payer (concentration + DSO risk)
- Revenue from top client masking decline in the rest of the book
- Company unable to raise prices with top client due to dependency

CONNECTIONS TO OTHER METRICS
- High concentration + declining revenue = likely that top client
  is reducing spend — investigate immediately
- High concentration + high DSO = the client that owes the most
  money is also the one you can least afford to lose
- High concentration + low churn overall = misleading health signal;
  churn of one client could be catastrophic
- High concentration + strong revenue growth = growth may be
  artificially inflated by one relationship, not real market traction

SAMPLE INTERPRETATION
A services firm reports top client concentration of 78.7%. This is
severely Dangerous. Nearly four-fifths of revenue depends on a single
client relationship. The adviser must treat this as an existential
risk, not just a diversification concern. Any deterioration in that
relationship — budget cuts, change in client leadership, competitive
displacement — would be immediately life-threatening to the business.
The primary strategic recommendation is aggressive new business
development to reduce this dependency below 40% within 12 months.
