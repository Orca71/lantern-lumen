METRIC: Monthly Revenue Trend
CATEGORY: Growth

DEFINITION
Monthly revenue trend tracks the direction and velocity of a company's
revenue over time. It answers the most fundamental business question:
is this company growing, shrinking, or stagnating? In professional
services, revenue trend is often a leading indicator of future
profitability and client health, since services revenue is directly
tied to active client relationships.

HOW IT'S CALCULATED
Month-over-Month Growth = ((Current Month Revenue - Prior Month Revenue)
                           / Prior Month Revenue) × 100

Year-over-Year Growth = ((Current Period Revenue - Same Period Last Year)
                         / Same Period Last Year) × 100

Year-over-year is more reliable for services firms because monthly
revenue can be lumpy due to project-based billing cycles.

BENCHMARKS — PROFESSIONAL SERVICES INDUSTRY
Strong Growth:    Above +15% YoY
Healthy Growth:   +5% to +15% YoY
Stable:           -5% to +5% YoY
Concerning:       -5% to -15% YoY
Critical Decline: Below -15% YoY

WHAT IT TELLS THE ADVISER
- Whether the company is winning new business faster than losing old
- Whether the sales pipeline is healthy
- Whether existing clients are expanding or contracting engagements
- Whether the business can sustain its current cost structure

WARNING SIGNS
- Consistent monthly decline for 3 or more consecutive months
- Year-over-year decline accelerating (getting worse each quarter)
- Revenue declining while headcount stays flat (productivity collapse)
- Revenue highly volatile month to month with no seasonal explanation
  (unstable client base or inconsistent billing practices)
- Single large project ending creates a sudden revenue cliff

CONNECTIONS TO OTHER METRICS
- Declining revenue + high fixed expenses = margin compression incoming
- Declining revenue + high client concentration = one lost client
  could cause the entire decline
- Declining revenue + rising DSO = clients reducing spend AND
  paying slowly — a serious double warning
- Declining revenue + low churn = clients are staying but spending less
  (scope reduction problem, not retention problem)

SAMPLE INTERPRETATION
A services firm shows revenue declining 22% year-over-year. This is
Critical Decline territory. The adviser should immediately investigate
whether this is concentrated in one or two lost clients (client
concentration and churn metrics) or spread broadly across the book
of business (a market or positioning problem). A 22% YoY decline
means the company is likely burning cash and will need to either
cut costs aggressively or accelerate new business development
within the next two quarters.
